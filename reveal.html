How attackers do this

  1. They place a malicious QR code or link where you expect something legitimate (posters, tables, emails).
  2. The QR sends you to a look‑alike login page.
  3. When you enter credentials, the site immediately collects them and may even redirect you to a real site to avoid suspicion.

Protect yourself

  • Inspect the URL before entering any credentials. Look for misspellings and unexpected domains.
  • Use a password manager — it won’t auto‑fill on impostor sites.
  • Turn on multi‑factor authentication (MFA) everywhere possible.
  • Be wary of public QR codes; prefer typing known URLs or using official apps.
